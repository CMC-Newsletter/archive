Taking that final 'L' of 2018 😡
Unfortunately, the last day of 2018 was a red one from a price action perspective. Total market capitalization gradually dropped ~$2.8 billion to ~$125.8 billion, with most coins seeing losses in the ~2-6% range. 😔

BTC also gradually fell from ~$3,850 to ~$3,750, and is now trading below its 21-day EMA line on the daily chart. Its RSI levels have remained relatively neutral, hovering in the ~47-48 range as of late, indicative of market indecisiveness. 🤜🤛 This is further evidenced by the current consolidation in this ~$3,800-$4,100 range.

Locked and loaded 🔫
Intercontinental Exchange (ICE)’s startup Bakkt has raised $182.5 million to build out its global digital assets platform and Bitcoin futures product. 🙌 Investors in the funding round (which is Bakkt’s first) included Boston Consulting Group, Galaxy Digital, Goldfinch Partners, ICE, M12 (Microsoft’s VC fund), Pantera Capital and Protocol Ventures. 😲

In a separate announcement, Bakkt disclosed that its planned Bitcoin futures market has been delayed again after consultation with the U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission. It had been expected to launch on Jan. 24. A new date will be announced early 2019. We saw this coming a mile away! 👀
Ripple effect 🌊
The National Bank of Kuwait (NBK) is utilizing Ripple (XRP)’s blockchain-based payments network to seek faster cross-border money transfers. The new remittance service, dubbed "NBK Direct Remit", enables “immediate” blockchain-based payments through Ripple technology. 😲

Presently, the service is launching with a remittance corridor to Jordan only, but is expected expand to more countries in the near future. 👍

NBK joins the growing number of financial institutions worldwide to have partnered with Ripple for its blockchain-based payments services. Most recently, these include Malaysian banking group CIMB, South Korea’s crypto exchange Coinone, U.S. banking giant PNC, and remittance firm UAE Exchange, among others. 👐
Just for you 👐
The cryptocurrency mining arm of Huobi Group, Huobi Pool, is launching a dedicated EOS exchange early in 2019 that will allow users to trade EOS against “a number” of yet to be revealed cryptocurrencies. It will also have a voting platform for EOS owners and manage an EOS community to raise awareness for node elections. 🗳️

Huobi Group’s cryptocurrency exchange is currently 3rd in our site's rankings by adjusted trade volume and according to them, the company is now achieving an accumulated turnover of over $1 trillion. 👌
Other news 📰
  • Peer-to-peer Bitcoin exchange Paxful saw a 130% increase in transactional volume growth in 2018. It also saw its user base in Ghana and Nigeria, tripling and doubling respectively. 💪
  • The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) of the UK recently disclosed that 18 companies are being investigated over their involvement in selling cryptocurrencies. 🧐
  • A recent survey conducted by Chinese blockchain news site PANews found that although 63% of the 4,200 people surveyed think cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin are unnecessary as a payment system, 40% of them would consider investing in cryptos. 🤷
