Over the weekend, total market capitalization bounced within ~$177 billion and ~$188.3 billion. BTC dominance is currently at ~55.8%, the highest it's been since mid September last year, indicative of the preference of the safer assets (less volatile, higher market capitalization) in the space. 

BTC is currently trading at ~$5,700. It experienced a run up from ~$5,500 to ~$5,800 on Friday evening and bounced between the ~$5,650-$5,850 range for the remainder of the weekend. On the weekly chart, BTC finally had 2 successive closes above the 200-day EMA line, a feat it has not been able to accomplish since mid May last year. Also on the weekly chart, RSI level is the highest it's been (~64) since January last year. BTC is now attempting to break through the very heavy resistance zone of ~$5,800-$6,400. 

Hearsay 👂

According to a recent report by The Wall Street Journal, "citing people familiar with the matter", Facebook is in talks with firms like Visa and Mastercard to support its planned stablecoin-based payments platform, and has already been working on it for about a year. 😊

The project, internally dubbed "Project Libra", seeks to develop a cryptocurrency that would enable the firm's billions of users to send money to each other, as well as make online purchases. Facebook has also been reportedly seeking to raise about $1 billion in total as collateral for the stablecoin to buffer it against volatility, and have been in discussions with e-commerce companies to gain acceptance of the planned stablecoin. 🙏

Notably, Facebook is said to be aiming to eliminate the swipe and card processing fees paid by merchants on every transaction to banks and payments processors and networks. 😲
Show me the money 🧐
According to a recent memorandum of law, the New York State Attorney General (NYAG), Letitia James, has requested the release of documents concerning the alleged deal made between crypto exchange Bitfinex and stablecoin operator Tether. 🧐

The memorandum of law claims “that Bitfinex and Tether misled their clients and investors”, and explains that the discrepancies in the case "only heighten the OAG’s [Office of the Attorney General’s] need to obtain documents and information in a timely, organized fashion so that the OAG may understand what has taken place, and what continues to take place, at these companies.” 🤔

At this point, authorities have also crafted an injunction to prevent them from further dissipating the cash reserves that supposedly back the tether virtual currency, pending completion of the OAG's investigation. 
Hmmm 🤔
A shareholder of crypto exchange Bitfinex, Zhao Dong, has released promotional material for the exchange's upcoming $1 billion initial exchange offering (IEO). 🗣️

The material, in the form of a document, allegedly released by the parent company behind Bitfinex and Tether, iFinex, announces the availability 1 billion USDT worth of the "LEO" for purchase. LEO will supposedly be used as the utility token of the Bitfinex ecosystem. 🤣

The marketing document promises LEO token holders reduced crypto to crypto trading fees, lending fees, withdrawal and deposit fees and derivatives fees. It also promises similar benefits on future iFinex trading platforms.
