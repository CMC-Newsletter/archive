Building pillars of support
The U.S. SEC is launching a "Strategic Hub for Innovation and Financial Technology (FinHub)" division to act as a central point for the securities regulator to monitor and interact with relevant representatives of the financial technology world, with an emphasis in the realms of distributed ledger technology, automated investment advice, digital marketplace financing and artificial intelligence. 😲

The Finhub will be run by the SEC's senior advisor for digital assets, Valerie Szczepanik, and will propagate information from the SEC and serve as a platform for innovators to ask questions and to clarify regulations. It will also work with overseas regulators on issues pertaining to emerging technologies. Overall, the Finhub seeks "to provide a clear path for entrepreneurs, developers and their advisers to engage with SEC staff, seek input and test ideas." 😄
Drafting guidelines
China's central government internet censor, the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC), has published a draft policy on blockchain startup regulation called "The Regulation for Managing Blockchain Information Services". 📜

The draft defines blockchain information service providers as "entities or nodes" that offer information services to the public, and requires them to register with the CAC within 10 days after it starts to offer products to the public. 🤔

The proposed draft also requisites blockchain startups to register their names, service types, industry fields, and server addresses with the CAC, who will then conduct annual reviews and make all these information publicly available. Information or content that is prohibited by Chinese laws will also not be allowed to be "produced, duplicated, published, and disseminated" by blockchain technology. ✋
Risk awareness
According to a report from cybersecurity vendor Group-IB, the infamous North Korean hacking group, Lazarus, has managed to steal approximately $571 million in cryptocurrencies. 💸

The report states that Lazarus is responsible for 14 crypto exchange hacks dating back from Jan 2017. The report also revealed that $882 million in cryptocurrencies was stolen from exchanges from 2017 to 2018 and anticipates the figure to grow due to hackers from the traditional finance realm becoming increasingly lured by the large gains in the cryptocurrency hacking space. 😠

The report also mentioned that all hackers have made off with about the 10% equivalent of the funds raised by ICO platforms since early 2017. Their modus operandi include spear phishing, social engineering and malware. Be careful out there guys! 🧐
Other news
  • The next hard fork of Ethereum (ETH), "Constantinople", that was initially set to be rolled out next month, will be postponed until early 2019. Several bugs were found in the code that was released on a test network prior to the initially next month roll out. 😭
  • The Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKEX) has proposed that fintech firms, including those related to blockchain and cryptocurrency, are best regulated under existing financial regulations. 👨‍⚖️
  • The France-based intergovernmental body, the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), will begin publishing rules for international cryptocurrency regulation by June 2019. 👍
