Odds stacked against us 🎲
In the last weekend of 2018, we saw total market capitalization recover from ~$120.4 billion on Friday to ~$128.6 billion 📈, and BTC dominance drop 1% point from ~52.9% to ~51.9%. 

Over the weekend, BTC hovered within a $160 range of ~$3,800 and ~$3,960, and is currently trading at ~$3,820. It traded along its 21-day EMA line on its daily chart, and also held its RSI levels in the ~48 to ~49 levels. From a price action perspective, it wasn't too exciting a weekend. 😴

As we head into 2019, the overall momentum still remains with the bears and it is likely that we have still yet to find the bottom of this bearish cycle. 🐻 On the weekly chart, BTC is trading below all 21, 50, 100, and 200 EMA lines and RSI levels continue indicate persistent bearish momentum, In all, there is still no sign of a trend reversal for now. 😔

The A-team 💪
The Ministry of Economic Development of Italy has published a list of 30 experts that will work together to develop the nation’s blockchain strategy. 👬 They will attempt to identify the potential use cases for blockchain in the public and private services, and develop the necessary technical and regulatory tools to promote adoption. 

The pool of expertise spans across areas such as blockchain tech, business, academic research, computer science, and law. 🤓 Some of the more notable individuals of the newly formed team include Angiolini Giorgio (Italtel marketing portfolio head, UN INFO blockchain group member), Monaco Marco (blockchain competence center leader at PWC Italy), Pimpinella Martino Maurizio (Italian Association of Paying Services Providers President) and Vitale Marco (Quadrans Foundation president). 👀

According to Italy’s deputy prime minister, Luigi Di Maio, "Emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence and blockchain are destined to radically change our (their) lives, the society in which we (they) live and the economic and productive fabric of the country. We have to decide which way to go.” 📢
Same same but different 🧐
Singapore crypto exchange Switcheo has announced that it now supports the Alchemist launched dollar-pegged SDUSD stablecoin which will hold the value of $1 and be locked to NEO. 💲

The NEO/SDUSD pair is now made available for trading on the Switcheo Exchange. According to the official press release by Switcheo, SDUSD is the first fiat-pegged stablecoin with NEO, and will be stabilized at $1 through the collateralization of NEO smart contracts. 😲
Upheaval 🙃
According to Chinese media outlet Odaily, Beijing-based Bitcoin mining equipment manufacturer Bitmain is looking to relieve its CEOs Jihan Wu and Micree Zhan (representing interests from the Jenke Group) of their duties as part of the ongoing restructuring in Bitmain, which could also see an individual with the last name "Wang" assume the position. 😲

According to sources, just last month Jihan Wu was reportedly demoted from the board of directors to a ‘supervisor’ position. In that time, he also banded with Roger Ver of Bitcoin Cash (ABC) to wage a hash war that resulted between factions within the Bitcoin Cash community. Wu caused his company to assign its surplus Bitcoin mining power in support of the Bitcoin Cash variant ABC, an alliance which eventually cost Bitmain millions of dollars. As for Zhan, it is reported that he took the blame for Bitmain’s poor performance in Q3, and accepted that his management skills didn’t help the company much. 👀
