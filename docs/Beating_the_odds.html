Against all odds 👊

Creating your own 'luck' 💪
The security token startup that aims to digitize traditional securities products on a blockchain, Securitize, has raised nearly ~$12.7 million in a Series A funding round led by Blockchain Capital. 💰 Other investors include Coinbase Ventures, Ripple’s Xpring, OKEx’s OK Blockchain Capital, Global Brain and NXTP. 😲

Securitize plans to use the funding to streamline its investor relations, build up liquidity and compliance efforts, and maintain capital formation. Blockchain Capital's co-founder, Brad Stephens, will also be joining Securitize’s board of directors. 🤝

Securitize CEO, Carlos Domingo, believes that "the tokenisation of legacy securities industry is taking place right now on a global scale", and thus wants to "take advantage of this emerging market opportunity" by growing Securitize's "engineering team and geographic coverage from Latin America to the Asia Pacific Region and other parts of the world." 👍
Pioneering efforts 👑
Ohio has become the first U.S. state to allow companies' taxes to be paid in Bitcoin. Ohio companies can now pay several taxes, such as tobacco sales tax, employee withholding tax and public utilities tax, with BTC. 🙌

Businesses first have to register with the Office of the Ohio Treasurer through an online portal called OhioCrypto.com, including its tax details such as payment amount and tax period. The due amount in BTC can then be paid through one of the “compatible” crypto wallets: Bitcoin Core client, Mycelium, breadwallet, and others that are compatible with the Bitcoin Payment Protocol. 🤖

Tax payments will then be processed by Atlanta-based Bitcoin payments processor BitPay, which will convert BTC to dollars for the Treasurer’s office. 💲
Taking a stand 🕴️
Cryptocurrency mining company, Bitmain, is facing a class action lawsuit for over $5 million. On 19 Nov, lead plaintiff and Los Angeles County resident Gor Gevorkyan filed the case against Bitmain in the federal court of the Northern District of California, alleging that the firm’s devices use customers’ resources to mine cryptos for its own benefit prior to full setup. 😠

According to the filed complaint, more than 100 class members are involved in the action and the aggregated amount under debate “exceeds $5,000,000, exclusive of interest, fees, and costs." 🤑

Gervorkyan purchased his Bitmain ASIC devices in January this year, but faced difficulties configuring and installing the devices. He claims that "the ASIC devices were pre-configured to mine and deliver crypto currency to Bitmain" and in that time "operated at full power mode, consuming a substantial amount of electricity." As a result, Gervorkyan and others are seeking “full restitution of all expenses incurred as a result of Bitmain’s unfair and deceptive practices.” 💸
