Setback 😔
The Cboe BZX Exchange has withdrawn its proposal for the VanEck-SolidX Bitcoin exchange-traded fund (ETF). On 22 Jan, the exchange filed its withdrawal. 😣

According to CoinDesk, the digital asset strategy director of VanEck, Gabor Gurbacs said that the filing “has been temporarily withdrawn” and that VanEck is still "working with regulators and major market participants to build appropriate market structure frameworks for a Bitcoin ETF and digital assets in general.”

Earlier in the week, VanEck CEO Jan van Eck told CNBC that the proposal was being withdrawn and would be submitted at a later date following continued discussions with the SEC. It was “engaged in discussions with the SEC about the Bitcoin-related issues, custody, market manipulation, prices", but had to stop due to the ongoing partial government shutdown in the U.S. Instead of trying to slip their Bitcoin ETF application through, it was decided upon to withdraw the application, and to "re-file when the SEC gets going again”. 
We're taking over 💰
Star Xu, founder of crypto exchange services provider OKCoin, has bought a controlling share in a Hong Kong Stock Exchange-listed firm for approximately $61,6 million. OKC Holdings Corp. is now the controlling shareholder in LEAP Holdings, a Caymans Island-incorporated construction engineering firm. 💪

OKC Holdings Corp. purchased roughly 3.2 billion shares at HK$0.15 (~$0.02) apiece — at a total cost of $483,890,536 (~$61.6 million), and now owns a 60.49% of Leap Holdings’ stock, with the same percentage of voting rights. Notably, Xu owns the majority stake in OKC Holdings (52.32 percent) through his 2 wholly-owned firms, StarXu Capital and OKEM Services Company.

OKC Holdings Corp.’s acquisition has the apparent hallmarks of a reverse initial public offering (IPO).
The next step 👣
The Jamaica Stock Exchange (JSE) is planning to list security tokens as tradable assets for clients, after completing a 60-day live cryptocurrency trading pilot that saw participation from regulated broker-dealers, market makers and the Jamaica Central Securities Depository (JCSD). 👀

According to the managing director of JSE, Marlene Street Forrest, the pilot was “very smooth”, and so the exchange is "looking forward to moving to the next stage of the pilot which would eventually include the listing of security tokens.”
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - 51% Attack

If more than half the computer power or mining hash rate on a network is run by a single person or a single group of people, then a 51% attack is in operation. This means that this entity has full control of the network and can negatively affect a cryptocurrency by taking over mining operations, stopping or changing transactions, and double-spending (reusing) coins.
