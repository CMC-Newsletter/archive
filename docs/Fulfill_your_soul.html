Well played, Jamie Dimon 👏
According to CNBC, JPMorgan Chase (JPM) will be launching its own cryptocurrency in real-world trials. 😲

Dubbed "JPM Coin", the digital token is pegged 1:1 to a single U.S. dollar (similar to stablecoins) and will be used to instantly settle transactions between clients of its wholesale payments business. While only a tiny fraction of payments will initially be transmitted using the cryptocurrency, the trial signifies the first real-world use of a digital coin by a major U.S. bank. 👌🏻

According to JPMorgan’s blockchain lead, Umar Farooq, the JPM Coin will be able to replace wire transfers for international payments by large corporate clients and cut settlement times, provide instant settlement for securities issuance, and replace U.S. dollars held internationally by subsidiaries of major corporations using JPMorgan’s treasury services.
All in one kit 👌🏻
Chinese search engine Baidu has launched a blockchain platform, dubbed the "Baidu Blockchain Engine (BBE) platform", aimed to simplify the commercial deployment of decentralized apps (dApps). 👍

The platform, hosted on Baidu's "intelligent" cloud, is being touted as an operating system that allows developers to produce blockchain applications “just like mobile apps” through smart contract templates, dapp templates and other services aimed to make development fast and simple. 🔨

BBE offers 6 primary features, including a trusted computing environment, high performance and throughput, scalable storage, cross-chain interactions, “intelligent” smart contracts and smart contract security auditing. It is also built on the ABC (AI, Big Data, cloud computing) technology stack that allows customers to develop ways to commercialize blockchain tech. 
Cost cutter 💸
According to HSBC's chief operating officer of FX cash trading and risk management, Mark Williamson, the HSBC FX Everywhere platform has saved 25% through the digital ledger system it has been using “to orchestrate payments across HSBC’s internal balance sheets” over the last year.

According to him, HSBC now processes from 3,500 to 5,000 trades each day on FX Everywhere, with trades now totaling $350 billion. 🤯 He also stated that through this, it was demonstrated that it "is not a one-off proof of concept". Williamson believes that soon, a “significant” amount of internal money flows are likely to be settled on the DLT system.

Crypto deep dive: Cardano
  • Cryptocurrency: ADA
  • Market Capitalization Rank: 12th (~$1.05 billion)
  • Consensus: A Proof of Stake (PoS) called "Ouroboros"
How it happened ⚙️
  • Cardano was created in Sept 2017 by blockchain development firm Input Output Hong Kong (IOHK), led by the co-founder of Ethereum, Ethereum Classic, and BitShares, Charles Hoskinson. 
  • Cardano has been dubbed the "Japanese Ethereum" because of the strong support it received from Japanese investors during its private presale.
Why people love it 😘
  • It is a 3rd generation smart contract and dApps platform. It was built after knowing about the problems plaguing the earlier platform generations.
  • It has a 2-layer design, one for settling transactions (settlement layer) and another for smart contract processing (control layer). Keeping them separate might allow it to be more efficient in the long run than say the Ethereum blockchain, that does not have this distinction. This 2-layer design also allows for soft forks to be implemented.
  • It is completely open-source. The Cardano team is very transparent with decisions made for the blockchain through constant updates and posts.
  • It has an experienced team of engineers and researchers (about 30 developers). It's GitHub page is one of the most active ones in crypto.
Why people doubt it 🤔
  • Cardano's adoption has not been the fastest, and so with more infrastructure coins popping up, this might prove to be a huge obstacle for them going forward. It also has to deal with Ethereum's overwhelmingly large first mover advantage.
  • Cardano's programming language for writing smart contracts, "Plutus", is still an unproven language. This uncertainty could further slow down its adoption as it might take some time to win the trust of potential adopters.
  • Cardano's current supply is rather centralized. The top addresses hold a large % of all ADA (about 75%).
