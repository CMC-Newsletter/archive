Major moves
On 11 Oct, the largest cryptocurrency exchange in South Korea, Bithumb, sold about 38% of its total ownership to BK Global Consortium, a blockchain investment firm formed by BK Global, a plastic surgery medical group in Singapore, for $350 million. 💰

BK Global Consortium is buying over the 38% stake from the current largest stakeholder of Bithumb, BTC Holdings Company, who agreed to sell 50% + 1 share of its current 76% stake. Hence. this move will make BK Global Consortium the major shareholder of Bithumb once the transaction is executed. This acquisition deal is valuing Bithumb at about $880 million. 🤑
Milestone met
Coinbase has added its first Ethereum-based token, 0x (ZRX) to its trading platform, Coinbase Pro. 😄

Coinbase made an announcement that it would enable trading for ZRX/USD, ZRX/EUR and ZRX/BTC at around 5:00 A.M UTC on 11 Oct. While ZRX trading will be accessible for users in most jurisdictions, it would not be initially made available for residents in the state of New York. It would also not be available on the Coinbase iOS and Andrioid apps as of yet. 

This new ZRX listing on the Coinbase Pro platform marks the first of potentially many more ERC-20 tokens to be given support on Coinbase. 💪
Added support
The trading app for both cryptocurrencies and stocks which boasts of more than 6 million customers, Robinhood, has launched and is now available in Ohio. Residents in the state of Ohio can now trade various cryptocurrencies commission-free through the Robinhood app. Some of the cryptocurrencies available on the app include BTC, ETH, LTC, BCH, ETC and DOGE. 🙌

The state of Ohio now joins the likes of Arizona, California, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Iowa, Massachusetts, Michigan, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, New Jersey, New Mexico, Pennsylvania, Texas, Utah, Virginia and Wisconsin, who can enjoy commission-free crypto trading services through Robinhood.

Crypto deep dive: Tether
  • Cryptocurrency: USDT
  • Market Capitalization Rank: 8th
  • Type: Stablecoin - at type of cryptocurrency that is meant to hold stable values. For example, Tether (USDT) is pegged to the USD and 1 unit of USDT is meant to trade at $1 at all times. 
How it happened ⚙️
  • Tether was originally named "Realcoin" back in July 2014 by its co-founders Brock Pierce, Reeve Collins and Craig Sellars as part of a blockchain startup and first issued tokens in Oct that year. In November that year, CEO Collins renamed it to "Tether". By January 2015, Tether started trading on the cryptocurrency exchange Bitfinex. Turns out, the CEO of Bitfinex, Jan Ludovicus van der Velde, was also the CEO of Tether Holdings Limited
Why people love it 😘
  • USDT is stable and digitized, which allows it to be used as a global medium of exchange that can be easily used for cross-border transactions. 
  • Due to its nature (1:1 peg to USD), USDT can be used as a store of value due to its reduced volatility. For example, in countries with tighter crypto exchange regulations (withdrawals), users can simply convert their cryptos into USDTs and store them with exchanges until a solution is reached, instead of having to hold the more volatile cryptocurrencies.
  • Due to its stable price, USDT provides a reference point for users in the crypto community and as a measurement unit to compare market values. 
Why people doubt it 🤔
  • Tether has been criticized in the past for not actually being fully collateralized. It even ended its business relationship with its audit firm, Friedman LLP, back in Jan this year before it was supposed to undergo its audit.
  • Tether is facing increasing competition, some other USD stablecoins with greater levels of transparency. Some competitors include TrueUSD (TUSD), nUSD (NUSD) and Gemini Dollar (GUSD).
  • Tether has been rumored to have been manipulating/ protecting the price of BTC during downturns by using USDT to purchase BTC to create artificial price floors.
