Low volatility 💤
Over the weekend, total market capitalization stayed within a tight range of ~$139.4 billion and ~$141.1 billion, and averaged a daily trading volume of ~$30 billion. The notable mover of that time would be Binance Coin (BNB), currently up ~14.8%, bringing it to #7 in the overall market capitalization rankings.

Also over the weekend, BTC traded above the ~$4,000 mark and traded within a small range of ~$4,010 and ~$4,050. The 30-day Bitcoin Volatility Index currently stands at ~2.32% - the only time we were below this level this year was back in mid Feb when it reached ~1.96%. It is still trading below the 100-day EMA line on the daily chart, and the 50-day EMA line on the weekly chart. 

Collaboration 🤝

BMW Group Asia, Intel and Nielson have established corporate partnerships with Singapore government-supported blockchain accelerator Tribe. 🤝

The partnerships will see the firms sharing their knowledge and subject expertise in their respective fields with Tribe’s startups in order to help build an “inclusive” ecosystem “ready for industry 4.0.” 😲

Also just last month, Tribe partnered with Ethereum development studio ConsenSys to further the blockchain ecosystem in Singapore.
Easing them in 🤗
U.S. shipping firm UPS has partnered with e-commerce company Inxeption to develop a blockchain platform, Inxeption Zippy, that will work as an online catalog for businesses so as to facilitate business-to-business (B2B) sales. 🤝

The platform will take businesses through the step-by-step process of setting up an online site for the company, listing its products and achieve sales to other businesses using contract-specific pricing. Then through blockchain, shipments, transactions, and purchase orders can then be scheduled and monitored on the Zippy platform. 🤖
Eastbound 👉
Bain Capital backed Chicago-based crypto exchange, Seed CX, is expanding to Asia. 😀

The exchange has teamed up with Singapore-based trading infrastructure technology provider, Hydra X, to integrate its platform with Hydra X’s trading platform, Sigma. Users will then be able to view prices, trade and monitor their portfolios on the Sigma platform.

According to Hydra X CEO, Daryl Low, “this partnership will also be useful in expanding our [Hydra X's] user base to include institutional traders seeking regulated and secure access to the world of digital assets”.
