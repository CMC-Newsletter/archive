It's picking up 😆
Payment processing company, Square, has revealed in its Q1 earnings report that it has seen strong growth in Bitcoin (BTC) sales through its Cash app, to the tune of ~$65.6 million in BTC revenue. 🤑

While costs were reported to be ~$64.7 million, the overall figures top previous all-time highs. In Q4 of 209, Square saw ~$52.4 million in BTC revenue and ~$490,000 in profit. As such, Q1 2019 profits achieved a 80% gain over the Q4 2018 profits. 

Nonetheless, BTC still remains to be a niche product for Square. The service is currently offered to all 50 states in the U.S. 👍
Alternative procurement method ✌️
Blockchain payments processor, Bitpay, has partnered with U.S.-based taxation services provider, Refundo, to provide Refundo customers with a service dubbed "CoinRT" that would allow them to receive their federal and state refunds in Bitcoin (BTC). 😲

Taxpayers first have to set up an account to get a unique routing and account number to input on their tax return, provide necessary know-your-customer (KYC) background information, and provide a BTC wallet address in order to use the service. Upon receiving tax refunds from the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), BitPay will automatically process the payment and send BTC to the registered BTC wallet address. It will cost $34.95, regardless of the refund amount.

According to Refundo CEO, Roger Chinchilla, the service seeks to help the underbanked community by providing them with a service to "get BTC quickly and easily for one flat fee” and avoid paying "high check cashing fees". 🙏
Influx anticipation 👀
According to the U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) chairman Christopher Giancarlo at a “the state of the CFTC” talk before the U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Agriculture Subcommittee, the "explosion in interest" in cryptocurrencies will likely give rise to new applications for clearinghouses. He also said that it's an area "in which protection of the cryptocurrencies will be one of the highest risks”. 👐

Clearinghouses are financial institutions that act as intermediaries to facilitate transactions between two parties and ensure trust. The CFTC routinely examines these entities to identify any possible issues that may affect their ability to monitor or control risk. 🧐

Giancarlo also stated that the CFTC will conduct increased examinations of clearinghouses to help them mitigate risks.
