Heeding advice 👍
Following recommendations from the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), the federal government of Pakistan has brought in Electronic Money Institutions (EMIs) draft regulations for its crypto industry. 📝

Under the new framework, companies are mandated to meet capital requirements, store personal details such as name, ID card number, address and telephone number as part of a due diligence process, as well as undergo executive evaluations in order to obtain licenses. ☑️

Through these draft regulations, the Pakistan government hopes to better monitor and regulate the sector, and prevent the illicit use of cryptocurrencies.
Meeting the demands 👐
Japanese crypto exchange Coincheck has launched an over-the-counter (OTC) crypto trading service, that will primarily deal with large Bitcoin (BTC) trades, to target larger clients. 🤖

Coincheck is the same exchange that suffered a $530 million hack in January 2018, losing about 500 million NEM tokens in the process. Shortly after in April that same year, the exchange was acquired by Japanese online brokerage Monex Group for $33.5 million. It has since received a regulatory license in Japan and is now a registered entity with the Kanto Financial Bureau.

The minimum trade size for Coincheck's OTC trading desk is 50 Bitcoins, and the service is available on weekdays between 10 a.m. (13 UTC) and 3 p.m. (18 UTC) Japan time.
Where there is smoke... 🤔
The Canadian police have frozen assets owned by the founders of blockchain services company Vanbex, Kevin Hobbs and Lisa Cheng, as part of a fraud investigation into a 2017 initial coin offering (ICO) that raised $22 million and saw the issuance of tokens under the ticker symbol, FUEL. 🤑

According to Canada’s Ministry of Attorney General, Vanbex told investors that the FUEL tokens would be usable in a forthcoming smart contract system called Etherparty, and that its value would "increase dramatically”, but then “developed no usable products”. 

Hobbs and Cheng then purchased 2 $3 million condominiums, one in Vancouver and the other in Toronto, 2 Land Rovers and leased a Lamborghini worth $375,000 for a 3-year term. Hobbs even spent some of the misappropriated funds on gambling “domestically and internationally at the high roller level”. 💸

The Canadian government has since seized the Land Rovers, ordered the Bank of Montreal to freeze Hobbs’ 2 accounts worth less than $1 million, and ordered him and Cheng not to sell, borrow against or damage their Vancouver condominium for at least 30 days.
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - Escrow
An escrow is a contractual arrangement in which a third party receives and disburses money or documents for the primary transacting parties, with the disbursement dependent on conditions agreed to by the transacting parties. This is possible to be automated using smart contracts on the blockchain.
