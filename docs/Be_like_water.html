Joint effort 🤝
According to a UAE official news agency, Emirate News Agency, The United Arab Emirates (UAE) and Saudi Arabia have announced an agreement to collaborate on the creation of a new cryptocurrency. 🤯

The Executive Committee of the Saudi-Emirati Coordination Council held a meeting Abu Dhabi that saw 16 attendees, to discuss the initiatives in the Strategy of Resolve. Among the 7 initiatives in the Strategy of Resolve, some include civil aviation, financial awareness youth training, and the development of a cross-border digital currency.

The new cryptocurrency “will be strictly targeted for banks at an experimental phase with the aim of better understanding the implications of blockchain technology and facilitating cross-border payments.” 👌🏻 This initiative seeks to protect customer interest, create standards for technology, consider the cybersecurity risks while determining the impact of central currencies on monetary policies and research the effect of a central currency on financial policies.
Heeded advice 🗣️
Netherlands’ Minister of Finance, Pete Hoekstra, has reportedly received official advice about cryptocurrencies from the Netherlands’ Authority for the Financial Markets and the local central bank, De Nederlandsche Bank, and has announced that he has begun working in accordance with the advice immediately after receiving it. ⚡

According to the article, the Hoekstra received advice that a licensing system should be introduced for crypto services, one that would require crypto exchanges and wallet providers to monitor their customers’ transactions and report suspicious activity to authorities. In addition, exchanges have to collect and store information about their customers in order to be able to hand it over to authorities in case of an investigation. 🤔

However, companies will first be tested before being granted the license to see if they are able to collect the required user data.
Changing conduct with the times 🌊
According to the Bangkok Post, the Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET) is planning to apply for a license from the country’s Ministry of Finance to launch a new digital assets exchange this year. 😲

According to the vice-chairwoman of the SET board of governors and chairwoman of the Association of Securities Companies, Pattera Dilokrungthirapop, this stems from the SET's desire to capitalize on the growing investor demand for cryptos. SET is now working to create a sound technical system for the exchange and a digital wallet for token storage. 😊

Pattera added that several securities company members of the SET already plan to apply to become brokers and dealers to trade on the new exchange.
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - Relative Strength Index (RSI)

A form of technical analysis that serves as a momentum oscillator, measuring the speed and change of price movements, developed by J. Welles Wilder. It oscillates between zero and 100, where a cryptocurrency is considered overbought when the indicator is above 70 and oversold when below 30. Don't use this as a standalone signal to buy though! All it really is, is a momentum gauge. It should be used in conjunction with other indicators before you decide to buy/sell.
