An ongoing battle ⚔️
Over the weekend, total market capitalization bounced within a tight range ~$175.5 billion and ~$181.8 billion, and maintained daily trading volumes of about ~$45 billion. 👀

Similarly, BTC traded within a tight range over the weekend and stayed between ~$5,240 and ~$5,360. BTC is currently trading at ~$5,300. As BTC continues to experience more consolidation at these levels (a sign of market indecisiveness), it also continues to be challenging the 0.618 fibonacci line (drawn from the mid Nov to mid Dec crash), a level it was rejected by just on 10 Apr. Concurrently, BTC is also testing the 200-day EMA line on the weekly chart, a level is has essentially been trading under since late Jan 2018. 

A healthy injection 💉
SBI Holdings Inc and FPT Corporation will be jointly investing $3 million in Vietnamese blockchain startup, Utop. They have both signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU). 💰

Utop is a technology startup based on FPT’s enterprise blockchain platform, dubbed akaChain. It's network allows for loyalty points to gained and redeemed at merchants. Also through the akaChain platform, registered small businesses will be able to reduce costs and secure data.

According to FPT Chairman, Truong Gia Binh, “the development of Industry 4.0 technologies has created breakthrough opportunities for businesses, mushrooming so many innovative business models. We call it digital transformation.”
Chilean crunch 💪
The Chilean government's Minister of Finance, Felipe Larraín, recently announced the introduction of a bill regulating cryptocurrencies and fintech. 📢

According to Felipe, the requirements introduced by the new regulation will be proportional to businesses. The new rules take into account the fact that various companies have varying business models, and as such offer different services that comes with differing risks for both users and the financial market. As a result, recognizing the rapid pace of technological progress in the space, flexibility became a key focus on the bill. 😊

Felipe also explained that "regulation of these platforms (crypto exchanges) would mitigate some of the risks, such as money laundering and terrorist financing, and increase the legal certainty with which they operate" and that they "want to adequately protect against the risks associated with this kind of activity.”
Closing shop 🏳️
South Korea's third largest crypto exchange by trading volume, Coinnest, has announced that it is closing down. 🏳️

On its website, there is a notice that requires users to withdraw any funds by 30 April, and that the fees for withdrawals and the minimum threshold have been lowered to assist the process. 

Pertaining to reasons for its closure, a Coinnest told crypto news source Coindesk (Korea) that “it is [was] a natural result of a decrease in trading volume. Both regulatory issues and business decisions have served as a background for this decision.” 💸
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - HODL
A type of passive investment strategy where you hold an investment for a long period of time, regardless of any changes in the price or markets. The term first became famous due to a typo made in a bitcoin forum, and the term is now commonly expanded to stand for “Hold On for Dear Life”.
