Weekend blip 😟
Amidst the festive Thanksgiving weekend, there was yet another flash crash in the crypto space. 😞 On Saturday, BTC broke past its $4,000 support level for the first time in 2018, which was then accompanied with a new low of ~$3,600. 😲 ETH also fell below $100, a level we haven't seen since May 2017. In that time, most cryptocurrencies were experiencing double digit losses and total market capitalization fell to ~$115 billion, marking a new low for the year.

On Sunday, markets then proceeded to recover and most cryptocurrencies experienced gains in the ~5-15% range, propping total market capitalization back up to ~$129.5 billion. BTC's RSI on its weekly chart remains in the oversold region of ~32, indicative of strong bearish momentum, and MACD indicators seem to imply that we are likely to see more bearish price action in the near future. 📉

Hush hush 🤫
Ethereum developers have been secretly discussing an upgrade, dubbed “Ethereum 1x”, that could quickly increase the short-term capabilities of the network, which could be implemented by June 2019. 😲

While it is still in early stages, meeting notes from 4 private meetings that occurred on October's Devcon4 conference suggest that Ethereum developers have discussed several changes to be included in a system-wide upgrade targeted for June, or hard fork, that will see changes not previously associated with a 2019 release. 👀

The series of upgrades potentially include replacing Ethereum’s underlying virtual machine (EVM) and introducing “rent,” or storage fees for smart contracts. 🤯
Green light 🚦
The Gibraltar Blockchain Exchange (GBX), Gibraltar Stock Exchange's blockchain subsidiary, has announced that it has been granted a license from the island’s financial regulator, the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission (GFSC), to own a regulated blockchain exchange. 🤝

The GFSC's enacted Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) regulatory framework this year made it compulsory for firms using blockchain to “store or transmit value belonging to others” to apply for a license. The Gibraltar government also plans to regulate initial coin offerings (ICOs), stating that most tokens are not considered securities under either Gibraltar or EU law. 🤔
The ousted 😵
According to Mao Shixing, the founder of F2pool, about 600,000 to 800,000 Bitcoin miners have ceased operations from mid-November due to a decrease in price and hashrate across the network. 😥

This estimate took into account the total drop in network hashrate and the average hash power of older mining machines that are struggling to make profits. Data from blockchain.info indicates that the Bitcoin network’s entire hashrate has dropped ~13% from around 47 million tera hashes per second (TH/s) on 10 Nov to 41 million on 24 Nov. 🐌

According to Mao, factors that contributed to the dropout rate include the recent 15 Nov Bitcoin Cash hard fork, rising electricity costs in China, and the continuous upgrading of products by Chinese manufacturers that make older machines increasingly obsolete.
