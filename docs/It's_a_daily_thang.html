A big four push 👐
In May, Big Four professional services firm EY will be rolling out free software design, dubbed "Nightfall", that will assist corporate clients in using the Ethereum blockchain. 😊

The protocol was in development over the past year by EY's consulting firm’s team of over 200 blockchain developers. It was created for supply chains, food tracing, transactions between branches of a company and public finance. 

Nightfall uses zero-knowledge proofs to enable private transactions on a shared ledger. However, Nightfall will be run on top of the public Ethereum network, not a private variant. 😲

EY is apparently open-sourcing the code of Nightfall, in an effort to "maximize adoption and community involvement", according to EY’s global innovation leader for blockchain, Paul Brody. He further explained that they "want people to adopt it, and adapt it, and improve it. If we [they] retain ownership, people may not invest that much time and energy in something they might not control'". 🙏🏻
Adoption driver 💪
Social investing platform eToro has launched a crypto exchange, along with 8 custom stablecoins. The platform is managed by eToroX, and is regulated by the Gibraltar Financial Services Commission. 

6 cryptocurrencies are offered at launch, namely Bitcoin (BTC), Bitcoin Cash (BCH), Ethereum (ETH), XRP (XRP), Litecoin (LTC), and Dash (DASH). The exchange will also offer crypto-fiat trading in 37 various pairs such as BTC/USD and BTC/GBP. The 8 eToro-branded tokens will be pegged to 8 various fiat currencies, such as the USD (USDEX), JPY (JPYX), EURO (EURX) etc. According to eToroX's managing director, Doron Rosenblum, the exchange will add more cryptocurrencies, stablecoins and tokens "in the coming weeks". 👌🏻

According to eToro CEO and co-founder, Yoni Assia, the “greatest transfer of wealth” will occur as financial services move onto the blockchain as the tokenization of traditionally illiquid assets such as art, property and intellectual property will occur. 🤑
New financial product 💰
Los Angeles-based digital asset manager, Arca, has filed a prospectus with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) seeking regulatory approval to sell shares of a new type of bond fund to retail investors. 🙏

The shares of the bond fund will be tokenized on the Ethereum blockchain, dubbed “Arca UST Coins”, and will be a type of stablecoin that will maintain parity with the U.S. dollar. However, Arca has cautioned that it may be slightly less stable than other such products on the market.

The minimum investment in the fund will be $1,000, with a target net asset value (NAV) of $1 per share. The fund will primarily invest in U.S. Treasury securities, and investors will receive quarterly dividends from the interest payments.
