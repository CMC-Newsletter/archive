Payment support 👐
Fortune 500 firm Avnet, known as a large distributor of electronic components and services, has partnered with blockchain payments processor BitPay and has started to accept crypto payments (namely Bitcoin (BTC) and Bitcoin Cash (BCH)) for its products and services. 👍

According to Avnet's vice president of demand creation, Sunny Trinh, Avnet is "working with BitPay to facilitate secure blockchain payments for all types of customers so they can focus on developing their products, not how to pay for them.” Also according to her, Avnet has already processed “several multi-million-dollar” cryptocurrency transactions in the first month of accepting crypto payments. 😲
Clientele growth 🤝
According to the IPO prospectus of Silvergate Bank, a U.S. financial institutions known to be actively serving cryptocurrency businesses, it has grown its clientele list by 59 in Q4 2018 but has seen overall deposits shrinking by 8% (~$123 million). 👬

The 59 clients added comprise of 2 exchanges, 24 investors and 33 firms. Well known clients include Kenetic, Polychain Capital and Genesis Trading. 💪

The reported decrease in deposits stemmed from exchanges, where deposits declined by ~$174.4 million, to ~$618.5 million (30 Sept to 30 Dec). On the contrary, crypto investor deposits increased ~$4.8 million to ~$577.5 million and other startups’ balances grew ~$46.4 million, to ~$273.9 million.
 
Prevention measures 👌🏻
According to Japanese news source, Nikkei Asian Review, the executive agency of the government of Japan known as the Japanese Cabinet has approved draft amendments related to financial instruments and payment services laws that affect cryptocurrencies. 📝

The first change is a cap on margin trading, using borrowed funds from a broker or an exchange to trade assets, that will be at 2-4x the initial deposit. The second change mandates all crypto exchanges in Japan that offer margin trading to register with the government within 18 months of the date the rules come into effect (expected to be Apr 2020).

According to Nikkei Asian Review, this law change is expected to protect investors from scams, and came to be in light of the rise in crypto margin trading in Japan in 2018 to 8.42 trillion yen ($75.6 billion), an amount that is 11 times greater than its crypto-to-cash conversions (777.4 billion yen or $6.9 billion). 😲
