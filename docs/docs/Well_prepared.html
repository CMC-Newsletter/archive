Goal in sight 🥅
In the past 24 hours, total market capitalization climbed from ~$120.2 billion to ~$121.2 billion, with most coins seeing gains in the ~1-3% range. Daily trading volume has been picking up as of late, and markets registered a ~$20.8 billion trading day yesterday. 👏

BTC is trading at ~$3,640, up ~0.4% over yesterday. Despite the recent run up, it remains to be trading below its 50-day EMA line on the weekly chart, a crucial level it previously held for the past few years until it broke down from it in mid Nov '18. BTC needs to clear this level to present a more credible case that the market has bottomed. 🧐

Global domination ✊
South Korean crypto exchange Bithumb is launching a licensed fiat-to-crypto exchange in the United Arab Emirates (UAE), and has signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the UAE-based firm Nvelop to collaborate on the initiative. 🤝

Nvelop is a firm that funds blockchain businesses in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. Through the partnership, Bithumb seeks to expand to MENA nations such as Saudi Arabia and Bahrain.
Mark your calendars ✍️
Crypto exchange Binance will soon be releasing its decentralized exchange, Binance DEX, for public testing. According to Binance CEO Changpeng Zhao, the firm is looking to launch the native public blockchain being developed to support the DEX, Binance Chain, on 20 Feb. 👀

Binance has already given early access to selected wallet developers and blockchain explorers, who will be integrating tools into the decentralized platform. For example, Ledger’s Nano S is already integrated in the platform. 👌🏻

Zhao also explained that the “Binance DEX works very similar to Bitcoin” in that "there’s no hidden transactions or private transactions.” He also said that there will be a $100,000 listing fee for tokens listed on Binance DEX to reduce the number of “spam or scam projects.”

Binance DEX will be available on all platforms, including Windows, Linux, Mac OS, iOS and Android. 🙌
Supply economics 🤜🤛
According to software giant, Oracle, it now has about a dozen live enterprise blockchain deployments including cargo tracking consortium Global Shipping Business Network (GSBN), educational and professional certificate sharer China Distance Education Holdings, conflict minerals tracker Circulor, and franchise invoice solutions provider SERES. 💪

Other Oracle customers currently in the production stage include Arab Jordan Investment Bank, Certified Origins, NeuroSoft, TradeFin, HealthSync, OriginTrail, ICS FS, SDK.Finance, and Nigeria Customs.

Prior to these deployments, the only major enterprise blockchains in production were built using the IBM Blockchain.
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - Airdrop

A marketing campaign that distributes a specific cryptocurrency or token to an audience. It is usually initiated by the creator of a cryptocurrency in order to encourage use and build popularity of the coin or token. Most airdrop campaigns run with mechanics such as receiving coins or tokens in exchange for simple tasks like sharing news, referring friends, or downloading an app.
