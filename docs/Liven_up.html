Command & conquer 💪
Crypto exchange Binance has launched a new fiat-to-crypto exchange on the island of Jersey, a British self-governing dependency, to target traders in Europe and the U.K. The exchange will enable users to trade Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH) against the British Pound (£) and the Euro (€). 😲

According to the CFO of Binance, Wei Zhou, the exchange selected Jersey for its "highly developed digital infrastructure, robust regulatory framework, and world-class financial services sector.” Zhou also clarified that Binance Jersey is a separate independent entity from its parent Binance.com, a crypto-to-crypto exchange, but is built using the same technology.

Binance has been working with Digital Jersey, a government-backed economic development agency, since Jun 2018 to develop the exchange and create about 40 jobs on the island. At the time, Binance's CEO Changpeng Zhao said that with Jersey's local economy being based on a major currency (GBP), and its close proximity to the U.K. and Western Europe, that it will help Binance "form a strong operational foundation for our (its) expansion into the rest of Europe.” 💪
Advanced tools 🔨
WordPress is launching a new publishing platform with inbuilt blockchain tools, "Newspack", backed by Google ($1.2 million), Ethereum development studio ConsenSys ($350,000) among others. 💰

Newsrooms that access Newspack will have the option to use blockchain-based features made available through ConsenSys-backed, Civil Media. Through Civil's "Publisher" product (that will be installed as a plugin on the WordPress platform), newsrooms will be able to permanently archive content on a decentralized network. 🤯

The plugin will also allow newsrooms to join the Civil Registry, a "hub of community vetted and approved newsrooms", and grant access to a “discovery portal” for consumers seeking news. 💪
Perseverance 😣
A bill to exempt companies providing non-custodial crypto services, from several state money transmitting laws, has been resubmitted to the United States Congress. 😀

The bill, titled “To provide a safe harbor from licensing and registration for certain non-controlling blockchain developers and providers of blockchain services,” was submitted by U.S. congressman Tom Emmer and co-sponsored by congressman Darren Soto. 🙌

It seeks to provide a "safe harbor from licensing and registration for certain non-controlling blockchain developers and providers of blockchain services”, so as to allow companies which use or trade cryptocurrencies but do not hold users’ coins to be exempt from money transmission laws if passed, on grounds that "only custodians present a risk of loss that would be sensibly addressed through licensing." 🤔
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - Proof-Of-Work (PoW)

A blockchain consensus mechanism involving solving of computationally intensive puzzles to validate transactions and create new blocks. PoW is the original consensus mechanism in a blockchain network, where miners compete with each other to solve these computationally complex puzzles and get rewarded when they are the first ones to do so.
