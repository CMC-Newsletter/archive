Cruise control
It seems like BTC is back to its usual low volatility routine. Its price fluctuated within a very tight range of ~$6,530 and ~$6,560, but ultimately did not change in price from yesterday and is currently trading at ~$6,550. Volume has also remained stagnant at the ~$4 billion levels over the past 3 days. RSI has also remained at ~57-58 levels, indicating low momentum. 30-day BTC/ USD  volatility is at ~1.92%, the lowest all year (less the start of Oct) - If not for the recent sell off and pump on Oct 10 and Oct 14 respectively, this figure would've dropped to about ~1.4%. 🤯

It was pretty much the same story with the rest of the other cryptocurrencies, and total market capitalization remained at ~$211 billion. Most cryptocurrencies saw either gains or losses in the ~1-4% range. 💤
First patron
The crypto merchant bank operated by billionaire and crypto enthusiast Mike Novograt, Galaxy Digital, has become the first crypto custody client of Fidelity Digital Assets of Fidelity Investments. 🤝

Less than 24 hours from its launch, Fidelity Digital Assets secured Galaxy Digital as its first client. Fidelity Digital Assets's founding head, Tom Jessop, said that that is "recognition that there is institutional demand" for cryptocurrencies and that family offices, hedge funds, other sophisticated investors should start "thinking seriously about this space." 💭

This partnership is expected to help clients of Galaxy Digital invest in the cryptocurrency markets through Fidelity Digital Assets. 🤑
Catching some wind
BTC futures trading at the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) has continued to grow in Q3. The CME has revealed that the average daily trading volume of BTC has increased by 41% percent in Q3 over Q2, while open interest (open/ outstanding contracts at any given time) on Bitcoin futures has risen by 19% in Q3. 📈

Overall trading on the futures market has been growing at a faster pace than in Q2. In Q3, the number of equivalent BTC traded for average daily volume was 5,053 BTC, compared to 3,577 BTC and 1,854 BTC in Q2 and Q1 respectively. Also in O3, the the number of equivalent BTC for open interest was 2,873 BTC, compared to 2,405 BTC and 1,523 BTC in Q2 and Q1 respectively. 😃
Picking up speed
The Amazon Web Services' (AWS) China division is partnering with blockchain project Qtum (QTUM) to develop blockchain-as-a-service (BaaS) solutions for both enterprises and developers. 🤝

Through the partnership, AWS users will be able to develop and launch smart contracts "quickly, efficiently, and cost-effectively" using an Amazon Machine Image (AMI). 👨‍🏫

The head of territory business development at AWS China, Simon Wang, has confirmed the partnership through an email to crypto news source, CoinDesk. He stated that "Qtum are now an AWS technology partner and one of the partner network members." 👌
Other news
  • Cobo, a Beijing-based cryptocurrency wallet startup founded by F2Pool's founder, has closed a $13 million Series A funding round in its bid to expand its services overseas. The likes of Danhua Venture Capital, the NEO Foundation and Wu Capital invested in this round. 💲
  • The country of Rwanda has launched a new blockchain initiative to track the supply chain of a metal commonly used in electronics, "tantalum." According to Rwandan cabinet minister Francis Gatare, the "new and innovative mineral traceability solution" has already been implemented "by at least one exporter from Rwanda." 💎
  • The Internet and Mobile Association of India (IAMAI) has formed a committee dedicated to blockchain technology. It seeks to engage with the government and startups to help develop the local blockchain ecosystem. The committee will be chaired by the digital chief of Mahindra Finance, Tina Singh. 💪
