Rebound 🏀
In the last 24 hours, total market capitalization rebounded from ~$168.6 billion to ~$175.7 billion, with most coins seeing gains in the ~3-7% range. Notable movers of the day include Bitcoin Cash (BCH) and Ravencoin (RVN), both up ~14.3% and ~19.7% respectively. 

BTC is currently trading at ~$5,390, up ~2.9% over yesterday. RSI level on the daily chart is currently at ~67.7. Many are speculating that the recent golden cross (50-day EMA line crossing to the upside of the 200-day EMA line, and usually seen as a bullish signal) signals the start of the next bull run. I believe that we need further confirmation for this, such as 2 successive closes above the 200-EMA on the weekly chart (something we have yet to see since mid Jan 2018) and breaking to the upside of the very heavy resistance zone between ~$5,800 and $6,800. As of right now, it seems that the recent 2019 run up is leaning more towards exhausting than towards persisting. 📝

Multi-pronged 🐙
Aspiring crypto derivatives provider, ErisX, has launched a spot market. While still in its initial phases, the public launch will see dollar trading pair support with Bitcoin (BTC), Bitcoin Cash (BCH), Litecoin (LTC) and Ethereum (ETH), and 3 BTC pairs. 

According to ErisX's chief strategy officer, Matt Trudeau, the platform will include both its exchange and clearinghouse, thus allowing it to take custody of both cash and cryptocurrencies. He added that “the spot market is really the first component of our overall corporate strategy of what we’re looking to do in 2019.” 👁️

Concurrently, ErisX is also seeking to provide crypto derivative offerings, which are still pending approval from the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC). According to Trudeau, they "are engaged in an active dialogue with the CFTC and appreciate the staff’s diligence in reviewing our application.” 😬
Facepalm 🤦‍♂️
According to documents released by its own lawyers following an inquiry by the New York Attorney General, it was revealed the company behind USD stablecoin Tether (USDT) only has enough cash to back ~74% of its supply. 😲

This figure fall short of previous promises given by Tether executives, in particular that every USDT token had full fiat backing, in which a released a bank statement appeared to confirm in December last year.

According to a statement by Zoe Phillips of law firm Morgan Lewis, “Tether’s reserves of cash and cash equivalents alone (without the line of credit) would cover approximately 74% of the outstanding amount of Tether”. She explained that "this sort of ‘fractional’ reserving arrangement is similar to how commercial banks work" and that "no bank holds in liquid cash more than a small percentage of depositors’ money". She also added that "the funds are invested". 🤦‍♂️
Industry gap 🕳️
In a recent seed round, Digital Assets Data received ~$6 million in funding to improve its enterprise-facing data services operation. Other investors include Digital Currency Group, Galaxy Digital, CMT Digital, Jump Capital, Morgan Creek, Ritholtz Wealth Management CEO Joshua Brown and others. 💰

Digital Assets Data provides analytical tools for crypto asset data, in hopes of solving poor data quality and the hassle of managing large quantities of data. They collect large quantities of on-chain, market, and reference data related to crypto assets and hosts them in a development environment. Firms can then access the data on the Digital Assets Data platform and code various analytical tools on it. 😄

According to its CEO, Mike Alfred, he does not "see enough investment [in crypto data analytics] relative to the opportunities" and thinks "it’s a multi-billion dollar market in the next three to five years”. 🤯
Blockchain term of the day 🤓 - Spot market
A public market in which cryptocurrencies are traded for immediate settlement. It contrasts with a futures market, in which settlement is due at a later date.
